Proj. 01 · 2024

Battersea
Pavilion

  • TypePublic Pavilion
  • LocationBattersea, London
  • Year2024
  • StatusAcademic Project

Project Description

A temporary public pavilion sited within Battersea Power Station's riverside envelope — exploring how exposed concrete and weathered steel can frame moments of stillness within a saturated urban redevelopment. The brief pushed for legibility without permanence.

Pavilion Concrete Public Space Battersea

Process Note

Structural logic derived from board-formed concrete panels — each joint a deliberate mark. Material research focused on in-situ casting techniques and the thermal mass of exposed slab construction in a temperate riverine climate.

// bio — plain text

I am an architecture student interested in the relationship between built form and lived experience — how space shapes behaviour, memory, and identity over time.

My work spans conceptual design, drawing, and material research, with a strong focus on adaptive reuse and the poetic realities of weathering, physical weight, and spatial compression.

This portfolio serves as a catalog of design questions, structural drafts, and temporary interventions built, imagined, or cast in situ.

For collaboration, critiques, or to request a full CV and printed portfolio, get in touch.
